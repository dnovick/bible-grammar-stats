## Etymology and Semantic Range

Compound: φίλος (loving) + αὐτός (self). NT hapax legomenon. Aristotle used the cognate φιλαυτία as the root of all vice (Eth. Nic. IX.8).

Placed first in the list, φίλαυτος appears to anchor the catalogue: all the vices that follow are in some sense expressions of disordered self-love. The Aristotelian background (self-love as the mother of vices) may be deliberate.

## Theological Note

Its position at the head of the list and its Aristotelian resonance suggest Paul is offering a diagnosis: the last-days vices are symptoms of a fundamental inversion — love of self displacing love of God (contrast φιλόθεος at the end, v.4).
