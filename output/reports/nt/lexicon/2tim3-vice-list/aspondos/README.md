## Etymology and Semantic Range

Alpha-privative + σπονδή (libation, treaty, truce). A σπονδή was the drink offering that sealed a treaty; ἄσπονδος is one who refuses to enter into any truce or cannot be reconciled.

NT: only 2 Tim 3:3 and Rom 1:31. The KJV "trucebreaker" slightly mistranslates: the term does not mean one who breaks an existing truce but one who is unwilling to make peace at all — implacable, perpetually at odds.

---

## OT / LXX Background

No LXX occurrences. Hebrew שָׁלוֹם (peace, wholeness) provides the conceptual background: the ἄσπονδος is the one incapable of שָׁלוֹם with others.

## Theological Note

The KJV translation "trucebreaker" has shaped much English preaching on this verse, but the underlying sense is more radical: not treachery toward an existing agreement, but refusal to seek peace at all.
