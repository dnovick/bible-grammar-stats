## Etymology and Semantic Range

From προδίδωμι (to hand over, betray): πρό (before/forth) + δίδωμι (to give). The one who "gives someone over" to their enemies.

Three NT occurrences: Luk 6:16 (Judas called προδότης — the only NT individual named a traitor), Act 7:52 (those who betrayed and killed the prophets), and here. Four LXX occurrences across 2 books.

---

## OT / LXX Background

Hebrew בֹּגֵד (treacherous one, traitor; Psa 119:158; Isa 24:16; Hab 1:13) — from בָּגַד (to act treacherously, to cover/wrap, hence to hide one's true intentions).

## Theological Note

The only named NT traitor is Judas (Luk 6:16). Paul's use here connects the church's last-days experience to the betrayal of the prophets (Act 7:52): a pattern of those inside the covenant community handing over the faithful.
