## Etymology and Semantic Range

Alpha-privative + κράτος (strength, power, control). NT hapax legomenon. Aristotle's term for the person who knows what is right but cannot control themselves enough to do it (Eth. Nic. VII).

No LXX occurrences. The cognate ἐγκράτεια (self-control) is a fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:23) and a virtue the Pastorals commend for elders (Tit 1:8). ἀκρατής is its direct opposite.

## Theological Note

The Aristotelian background is worth noting: classical virtue ethics considered ἀκρασία (incontinence) a fundamental character defect. Paul adopts the term but locates its remedy not in philosophical discipline but in the Spirit's fruit of ἐγκράτεια.
