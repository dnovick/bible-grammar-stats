Key reminder — Neuter Rule: In neuter nouns, nominative = accusative = vocative (both singular and plural). Context must determine function. · Article nom. forms: ὁ, ἡ, οἱ, αἱ — these four do not begin with τ. All others do.

Part C — Context Sentences

Parse the bold form and translate the whole sentence.

#SentenceBold Form: ParseTranslation
C1ὁ θεὸς ἀγαπᾷ τὸν κόσμον
τὸν κόσμον: Accusative singular masculine — article + noun, direct object of ἀγαπᾷ · Translation: "God loves the world" (cf. John 3:16)
C2ὁ λόγος θεὸς ἦν
θεός: Nominative singular masculine — predicate nominative; no article = qualitative ("what the Word is in nature/character") · Translation: "The Word was God" (John 1:1)
C3βλέπω τὰ ἔργα
τὰ ἔργα: Accusative plural neuter — article + noun, direct object of βλέπω · Translation: "I see the works/deeds"
