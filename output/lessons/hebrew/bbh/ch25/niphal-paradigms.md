# Niphal (נִפְעַל) — Full Conjugation Paradigms

*Model root: קטל (Q = קָטַל). All forms are strong-root paradigms.*
*Source: Basics of Biblical Hebrew, Pratico & Van Pelt, Ch. 23–25*

---

## Perfect (Qatal)

*BBH §23.3 — Prefix: נִ; characteristic vowel: patach under first root letter*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3ms | נִקְטַל | — |
| 3fs | נִקְטְלָה | נִקְטְלוּ |
| 2ms | נִקְטַלְתָּ | נִקְטַלְתֶּם |
| 2fs | נִקְטַלְתְּ | נִקְטַלְתֶּן |
| 1cs | נִקְטַלְתִּי | נִקְטַלְנוּ |

> **Key marker:** נִ prefix + *patach* under first root consonant. Distinguished from Hiphil perfect (הִקְטִיל) by נִ vs. הִ and the absence of the characteristic *chiriq* in the second syllable.

---

## Imperfect (Yiqtol)

*BBH §23.5 — Prefix vowel: patach (יִ); characteristic: tsere in final syllable*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3ms | יִקָּטֵל | יִקָּטְלוּ |
| 3fs | תִּקָּטֵל | תִּקָּטַלְנָה |
| 2ms | תִּקָּטֵל | תִּקָּטְלוּ |
| 2fs | תִּקָּטְלִי | תִּקָּטַלְנָה |
| 1cs | אֶקָּטֵל | נִקָּטֵל |

> **Key marker:** Doubled (dagesh forte) first root consonant + *tsere* in final syllable. The doubling of the first root letter (assimilation of the נ of the Niphal prefix) is the primary diagnostic of the Niphal imperfect.

---

## Wayyiqtol

*Sequential narrative form of the imperfect. Prefix: וַיִּ; same dagesh + tsere pattern.*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3ms | וַיִּקָּטֵל | וַיִּקָּטְלוּ |
| 3fs | וַתִּקָּטֵל | וַתִּקָּטַלְנָה |
| 2ms | וַתִּקָּטֵל | וַתִּקָּטְלוּ |
| 1cs | וָאֶקָּטֵל | וַנִּקָּטֵל |

> **Key marker:** וַיִּ prefix + doubled first root consonant + *tsere*. The assimilated נ (dagesh forte in the first root letter) is the same as in the yiqtol.

---

## Weqatal

*Sequential future/consequential form of the perfect. וְ + perfect form.*

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3ms | וְנִקְטַל | וְנִקְטְלוּ |
| 3fs | וְנִקְטְלָה | — |
| 2ms | וְנִקְטַלְתָּ | וְנִקְטַלְתֶּם |
| 1cs | וְנִקְטַלְתִּי | וְנִקְטַלְנוּ |

> **Key marker:** וְ + identical to the perfect paradigm. Context distinguishes it from a simple perfect with vav-conjunction.

---

## Imperative

*BBH §23.7 — Prefix: הִ; tsere in final syllable*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 2ms | הִקָּטֵל | הִקָּטְלוּ |
| 2fs | הִקָּטְלִי | הִקָּטַלְנָה |

> **Key marker:** הִ prefix + doubled first root consonant + *tsere*. The Niphal imperative looks similar to the Hiphil imperative (הַקְטֵל) but differs in the prefix vowel: Niphal הִ (*hiriq*) vs. Hiphil הַ (*patach*).

---

## Infinitive Construct

*BBH §23.9 — Prefix: הִ; tsere in final syllable*

| Form | Notes |
|---|---|
| הִקָּטֵל | Base form |
| לְהִקָּטֵל | With לְ preposition (purpose/complement) |
| בְּהִקָּטֵל | With בְּ (temporal: "when/while being done") |

> **Key marker:** הִ prefix + doubled first root consonant + *tsere*. Identical in form to the imperative 2ms; context and prepositions clarify function.

---

## Infinitive Absolute

*BBH §23.11 — Prefix: נִ; holem in final syllable*

| Form | Notes |
|---|---|
| נִקְטֹל | Standard form |

> **Usage:** Appears before or after a finite Niphal verb for emphasis (הִמָּצֵא יִמָּצֵא "he will certainly be found," Exo 22:3). Less common than the Qal infinitive absolute.

---

## Participle (Passive)

*BBH §23.13 — Prefix: נִ; tsere in second syllable*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| ms | נִקְטָל | נִקְטָלִים |
| fs | נִקְטֶלֶת | נִקְטָלוֹת |

> **Key marker:** נִ prefix + *qamets* under the second root consonant (ms). Distinguished from the perfect by the *qamets* vs. *patach*: נִקְטָל (participle) vs. נִקְטַל (perfect 3ms).
