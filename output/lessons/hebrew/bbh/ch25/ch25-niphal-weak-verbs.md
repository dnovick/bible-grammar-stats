# Chapter 25 — Niphal Weak Verbs (נִפְעַל)

*Basics of Biblical Hebrew, Pratico & Van Pelt*
*Data: MACULA Hebrew WLC (4,144 OT Niphal tokens)*

> **Context:** The Niphal is the third most common derived stem in Biblical Hebrew (5.7% of all OT verb tokens), after the Qal and Hiphil. See [OT Verb Stems Overview](../../../../reports/ot/verbs/ot-verb-stems-pie.md) for a full comparison.

---

## 1. Function (BBH §24.2)

The Niphal expresses **passive, reflexive, or middle** action. Unlike the Hiphil (which is consistently causative), the Niphal's function varies by root and context.

| Function | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Passive** | Subject receives the action of the Qal verb | נִמְכַּר "he was sold" ← מָכַר "to sell" |
| **Reflexive** | Subject performs the action on or for itself | נִשְׁבַּע "he swore" ← שָׁבַע "to bind by oath" |
| **Middle/Tolerative** | Subject allows or permits the action to happen | נִרְאָה "he appeared" ← רָאָה "to see" (lit. "he allowed himself to be seen") |
| **Simple action** | Niphal is the standard lexical form; no Qal counterpart | נִלְחַם "he fought" |
| **Reciprocal** *(not in BBH)* | Subjects act on one another | נִוָּעֲדוּ "they met together" ← יָעַד |

> **Key diagnostic:** Ask, "Is the subject *receiving* the action, *doing it to itself*, or *allowing it to happen to itself*?" The answer identifies the Niphal function type.

## 2. Form

### Diagnostic Markers

- **Perfect:** נִ prefix + *patach* under first root letter → נִקְטַל
- **Imperfect:** *hiriq* under prefix consonant + doubled (dagesh forte) first root letter + *tsere* → יִקָּטֵל
- **Wayyiqtol:** וַיִּ prefix + doubled first root letter + *tsere* → וַיִּקָּטֵל
- **Imperative:** הִ prefix + doubled first root letter + *tsere* → הִקָּטֵל
- **Infinitive Construct:** הִ prefix + doubled first root letter + *tsere* → הִקָּטֵל
- **Infinitive Absolute:** נִ prefix + *holem* → נִקְטֹל
- **Participle:** נִ prefix + *qamets* under second root letter → נִקְטָל

> **The dagesh diagnostic:** In the Niphal imperfect, imperative, and infinitive construct, the נ of the Niphal prefix *assimilates* into the first root consonant, producing a dagesh forte. This doubling is the clearest marker of the Niphal in those forms.

## 4. Example Passages

One example per semantic function × conjugation form. Torah passages preferred; other OT books used where Torah has no example of that combination.

### Passive

The subject receives the action of the corresponding active (Qal/Piel) verb.

**Perfect (qatal) — Gen 17:14** — וְנִכְרְתָ֛ה הַנֶּ֥פֶשׁ הַהִ֖וא
*"That person shall be cut off."*
→ כָּרַת Niphal perfect 3fs; the soul is the recipient of the cutting off — classic passive of the covenant-exclusion formula.

**Wayyiqtol — Gen 4:18** — וַיִּוָּלֵ֤ד לַֽחֲנוֹךְ֙
*"And to Enoch was born…"*
→ יָלַד Niphal wayyiqtol 3ms; the child is born (receives the action of begetting) — standard genealogical formula.

**Imperfect (yiqtol) — Gen 6:21** — וְהָיָ֥ה לְךָ֖ וְלָהֶ֥ם לְאָכְלָֽה יֵֽאָכֵ֔ל
*"It shall be food for you and for them — it shall be eaten."*
→ אָכַל Niphal imperfect 3ms; the food is the subject that will be consumed — impersonal passive.

**Weqatal — Gen 9:2** — וְנִתְּנ֖וּ בְּיֶדְכֶ֑ם
*"They are given into your hand."*
→ נָתַן Niphal weqatal 3mp; God declares that all creatures are placed under human authority.

**Imperative — Gen 24:6** — הִשָּׁ֣מֶר לְךָ֔
*"Take heed for yourself!"*
→ שָׁמַר Niphal imperative 2ms; Abraham warns his servant to be on guard (guard yourself = reflexive shade, but passive-reflexive overlap is common here).

**Infinitive Construct — Gen 21:5** — בְּהִוָּ֣לֶד ל֔וֹ
*"when Isaac was born to him"*
→ יָלַד Niphal infinitive construct with בְּ; temporal clause — the birth event as the frame of reference.

**Infinitive Absolute — Exo 22:3 (Eng. 22:4)** — הִמָּצֵא֩ יִמָּצֵ֨א בְיָד֙וֹ
*"If the stolen animal is actually found in his possession…"*
→ מָצָא Niphal infinitive absolute + imperfect; the doubling emphasizes certainty of discovery.

**Participle — Gen 47:14** — הַנִּמְצָ֤א
*"that which was found"*
→ מָצָא Niphal participle ms; attributive/substantival use — "that which was found [in the land]."

---

### Reflexive

The subject performs the action on or for itself.

**Perfect (qatal) — Exo 7:15** — נִצַּבְתָּ֥ לִקְרָאתֹ֖ו
*"station yourself to meet him"*
→ נָצַב Niphal perfect 2ms; Moses is told to place himself (set himself in position) at the river.

**Wayyiqtol — Gen 3:10** — וָאֵחָבֵֽא
*"And I hid myself."*
→ חָבָא Niphal wayyiqtol 1cs; Adam hides himself — the reflexive force is explicit in the English.

**Imperfect (yiqtol) — Gen 21:24** — אִשָּׁבֵֽעַ
*"I will swear."*
→ שָׁבַע Niphal imperfect 1cs; Abraham commits himself by oath — the Niphal of שָׁבַע means "to bind oneself by oath."

**Weqatal — Gen 21:31** — נִשְׁבְּע֖וּ שְׁנֵיהֶֽם
*"The two of them swore an oath."*
→ שָׁבַע Niphal weqatal 3mp; the covenant-making ceremony at Beersheba.

**Imperative — Gen 21:23** — הִשָּׁ֨בְעָה לִּ֣י
*"Swear to me!"*
→ שָׁבַע Niphal imperative 2ms; Abimelech demands a binding oath from Abraham.

**Infinitive Construct — 2 Chr 18:24** — לְהֵחָבֵֽא
*"to hide yourself"*
→ חָבָא Niphal infinitive construct with לְ; the closest post-Torah example of this reflexive root in infinitive construct. *(No Torah attestation for this conj × root combo.)*

**Infinitive Absolute — Num 30:3 (Eng. 30:2)** — הִשָּׁ֤בַע שְׁבוּעָה֙
*"swears an oath"*
→ שָׁבַע Niphal infinitive absolute + verbal form; emphatic oath-swearing construction in the vow law.

**Participle — Gen 18:2** — נִצָּבִ֖ים עָלָ֑יו
*"standing near him"*
→ נָצַב Niphal participle mp; Abraham looks up and sees three men positioned (having stationed themselves) nearby.

---

### Middle / Tolerative

The subject allows the action to happen or undergoes it with some degree of volition.

**Perfect (qatal) — Gen 3:5** — נִפְקְח֖וּ עֵינֵיכֶ֑ם
*"your eyes will be opened"*
→ פָּקַח Niphal perfect 3cp; the serpent's promise — eyes that will open (allow themselves to open) to knowledge.

**Wayyiqtol — Gen 3:7** — וַתִּפָּקַ֙חְנָה֙ עֵינֵ֣י שְׁנֵיהֶ֔ם
*"Then the eyes of both of them were opened."*
→ פָּקַח Niphal wayyiqtol 3fp; the fulfillment of the serpent's words — the eyes opened (underwent opening).

**Imperfect (yiqtol) — Gen 1:9** — תֵרָאֶ֖ה הַיַּבָּשָׁ֑ה
*"Let the dry land appear."*
→ רָאָה Niphal imperfect/jussive 3fs; God commands the land to allow itself to be seen — the tolerative sense is clear.

**Weqatal — Gen 8:5** — וְנִרְא֖וּ רָאשֵׁ֥י הֶהָרִֽים
*"and the tops of the mountains were seen."*
→ רָאָה Niphal weqatal 3mp; as the waters receded, the mountaintops became visible.

**Imperative — 1 Kgs 18:1** — הֵרָאֵ֣ה אֶל־אַחְאָ֑ב
*"Go, present yourself to Ahab."*
→ רָאָה Niphal imperative 2ms; Elijah is commanded to allow himself to be seen. *(No Torah imperative for this root; 1 Kings is earliest attestation.)*

**Infinitive Construct — Exo 34:24** — לְהֵרָאוֹת֙ אֶת־פְּנֵ֣י
*"to appear before the face of…"*
→ רָאָה Niphal infinitive construct; the male Israelites shall present themselves (allow themselves to be seen) before God three times a year.

**Infinitive Absolute — 2 Sam 1:6** — נִקְרֹ֤א נִקְרֵ֙יתִי֙
*"I happened to be…"*
→ קָרָא Niphal infinitive absolute + perfect; the young man describes finding himself in a certain place — the middle force ("I found myself there").

**Participle — Gen 12:7** — וַיֵּרָ֤א יְהוָה֙ אֶל־אַבְרָ֔ם … הַנִּרְאֶ֥ה אֵלָֽיו
*"The LORD appeared to Abram… who had appeared to him."*
→ רָאָה Niphal participle ms; God as the one who presents himself to be seen — the participial form substantivizes the appearance.

---

### Simple Action (Niphal-Only)

These roots have no active Qal meaning; the Niphal form is the standard lexical entry.

**Perfect (qatal) — Exo 1:10** — וְנִלְחַם־בָּ֖נוּ
*"they will fight against us"*
→ לָחַם Niphal perfect 3ms (in a conditional/future context); Pharaoh fears the Israelites will join enemies in battle — לָחַם occurs only in the Niphal.

**Wayyiqtol — Gen 6:6** — וַיִּנָּ֣חֶם יְהוָ֔ה
*"And the LORD regretted."*
→ נָחַם Niphal wayyiqtol 3ms; God's emotional response to human wickedness — נָחַם Niphal = "to regret, relent, be comforted."

**Imperfect (yiqtol) — Exo 13:17** — פֶּ֖ן יִנָּחֵ֥ם הָעָ֖ם
*"lest the people regret / change their minds"*
→ נָחַם Niphal imperfect 3ms; God reroutes Israel to prevent them from having second thoughts about leaving Egypt.

**Weqatal — Gen 6:7** — נִחַ֖מְתִּי כִּ֥י עֲשִׂיתִֽם
*"I regret that I made them."*
→ נָחַם Niphal weqatal 1cs; God's soliloquy before the flood — divine grief expressed as self-directed relenting.

**Imperative — Exo 17:9** — הִלָּחֵ֣ם בַּֽעֲמָלֵ֑ק
*"Fight against Amalek!"*
→ לָחַם Niphal imperative 2ms; Moses commands Joshua to engage in battle — the only way to express "fight!" in Biblical Hebrew.

**Infinitive Construct — Exo 17:10** — לְהִלָּחֵ֖ם בַּעֲמָלֵֽק
*"to fight against Amalek"*
→ לָחַם Niphal infinitive construct with לְ; Joshua did as Moses told him in going out to fight.

**Infinitive Absolute — Jdg 11:25** — הֲנִלְחֹ֥ם נִלְחַ֖ם בְּיִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל
*"Did he ever actually wage war against Israel?"*
→ לָחַם Niphal infinitive absolute + perfect; Jephthah's rhetorical challenge — the doubling emphasizes the question.

**Participle — Exo 14:25** — כִּ֥י יְהוָ֖ה נִלְחָ֥ם לָהֶֽם
*"for the LORD is fighting for them"*
→ לָחַם Niphal participle ms; the Egyptians' terrified recognition at the Red Sea — a participial predicate.

| Weqatal | 1,088 | 26.3% |
| Imperfect (yiqtol) | 1,024 | 24.7% |
| Participle | 558 | 13.5% |
| Wayyiqtol | 443 | 10.7% |
| Perfect (qatal) | 328 | 7.9% |
| Infinitive Construct | 206 | 5.0% |
| Imperative | 118 | 2.8% |
| Jussive | 63 | 1.5% |
| Infinitive Absolute | 35 | 0.8% |

*Total OT Niphal tokens: 4,144 across 39 books.*

> The weqatal and imperfect together account for over 50% of all Niphal tokens — reflecting heavy use in legal and prophetic texts where conditions and consequences are stated ("if X is done…", "it shall be cut off"). The high participle count (13.5%) reflects the Niphal's frequent use as a substantive ("the one who is…").
