# Piel and Pual Paradigm Tables — דִּבֵּר / קִדֵּשׁ

*Piel paradigm root: דִּבֵּר (to speak) — strong triliteral, denominative*
*Pual paradigm root: קֻדַּשׁ (to be consecrated) — passive of Piel קִדֵּשׁ*

---

## Perfect (Qatal)

| Person | Qal (ref.) | **Piel** (דבר) | **Pual** (קדש) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3ms | קָטַל | **דִּבֵּר** | **קֻדַּשׁ** |
| 3fs | קָטְלָה | **דִּבְּרָה** | **קֻדְּשָׁה** |
| 2ms | קָטַלְתָּ | **דִּבַּרְתָּ** | **קֻדַּשְׁתָּ** |
| 2fs | קָטַלְתְּ | **דִּבַּרְתְּ** | **קֻדַּשְׁתְּ** |
| 1cs | קָטַלְתִּי | **דִּבַּרְתִּי** | **קֻדַּשְׁתִּי** |
| 3cp | קָטְלוּ | **דִּבְּרוּ** | **קֻדְּשׁוּ** |
| 2mp | קְטַלְתֶּם | **דִּבַּרְתֶּם** | **קֻדַּשְׁתֶּם** |
| 2fp | קְטַלְתֶּן | **דִּבַּרְתֶּן** | **קֻדַּשְׁתֶּן** |
| 1cp | קָטַלְנוּ | **דִּבַּרְנוּ** | **קֻדַּשְׁנוּ** |

> **Key marker — Piel:** Hireq (ִ) under R1 + **Dagesh Forte in R2** + Tsere (ֵ) under R2 in 3ms/3fs. The dagesh in R2 is the single most reliable Piel/Pual identifier across all conjugations.
>
> **Key marker — Pual:** Qibbuts (ֻ) or Shureq under R1 + **Dagesh Forte in R2** + Patach under R2 (3ms). The Pual is always passive: u-vowel under R1 + dagesh in R2.

> **Key marker — Piel:** Shewa under prefix + **Patach under R1** + dagesh in R2 + Tsere under R2 (3ms). Prefix vowel is shewa (יְ, תְּ), except 1cs אֲ.
>
> **Key marker — Pual:** Same prefix pattern as Piel but **Qibbuts under R1** and dagesh in R2.

## Infinitive Absolute

| Form | **Piel** | **Pual** |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | **דַּבֵּר** | **קֻדַּשׁ** |

> **Key marker:** Piel Inf. Absolute = same form as Imperative/Inf. Construct. Used in cognate constructions: דַּבֵּר יְדַבֵּר "he will certainly speak."

## Participle

| Form | **Piel (active)** | **Pual (passive)** |
|---|---|---|
| ms | **מְדַבֵּר** | **מְקֻדָּשׁ** |
| fs | **מְדַבֶּרֶת** | **מְקֻדֶּשֶׁת** |
| mp | **מְדַבְּרִים** | **מְקֻדָּשִׁים** |
| fp | **מְדַבְּרוֹת** | **מְקֻדָּשׁוֹת** |

> **Key marker — Piel participle:** מְ prefix + Patach under R1 + dagesh in R2 + Tsere under R2 (ms). The מְ prefix is shared with Pual and Hiphil participles — the dagesh in R2 distinguishes Piel/Pual from Hiphil (no dagesh in R2).
>
> **Key marker — Pual participle:** מְ prefix + **Qibbuts/Qamets under R1** + dagesh in R2. The passive vowel under R1 identifies the Pual.

## Summary: Qal vs. Piel vs. Pual at a Glance

| Conjugation | Qal marker | **Piel marker** | **Pual marker** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Perfect 3ms | קָטַל | **דִּ + dagesh + Tsere** | **קֻ + dagesh + Patach** |
| Imperfect 3ms | יִקְטֹל / no dagesh | **יְ + Patach + dagesh** | **יְ + Qibbuts + dagesh** |
| Wayyiqtol | וַיִּ prefix | **וַיְ prefix** | **וַיְ prefix (passive)** |
| Weqatal | וְקָ | **וְדִ** | **וְקֻ** |
| Imperative 2ms | no prefix | **דַּ + dagesh + Tsere** | — (no Pual imperative) |
| Inf. Construct | קְטֹל | **דַּבֵּר** | rare |
| Inf. Absolute | קָטוֹל | **דַּבֵּר** | **קֻדַּשׁ** |
| Participle ms | קֹטֵל | **מְדַבֵּר** | **מְקֻדָּשׁ** |

**The single most reliable Piel/Pual identifier: Dagesh Forte in R2 (the middle root consonant).**
