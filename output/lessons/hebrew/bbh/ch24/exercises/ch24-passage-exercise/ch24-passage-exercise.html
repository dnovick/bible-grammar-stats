Reflection Questions
  1. Several Niphal forms of אָסַף in this passage describe Jacob's death (Gen 49:29, 33). What does the passive "to be gathered to one's people" suggest about ancient Israelite views of death?
  2. In Gen 47:31, both הִשָּׁ֣בְעָה (imperative) and וַיִּשָּׁבַע (wayyiqtol) appear together. How does the narrative sequence reinforce the solemnity of Jacob's deathbed request?
  3. Compare נִצָּבָה in Gen 37:7 (Reflexive — sheaf stands itself upright) with הַנִּצָּבִים in Gen 45:1 (Reflexive participle — servants stationed before Joseph). Is the reflexive force the same in both? What does each communicate about agency?
