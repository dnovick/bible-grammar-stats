# Chapter 24 — "Spot the Niphal" Passage Exercise

*Genesis 37, 44, 45, 47, and 49: The Joseph Narrative*

---

## Instructions

The passages below are drawn from the Joseph story (Genesis 37–50), one of the richest concentrations of Niphal verbs in the Torah. The narrative includes passive birth and sale notices, reflexive oath-swearing, and simple-action verbs with no Qal counterpart.

**For each numbered verb, answer: Is it Niphal? (Yes / No). Then for every verb:**

1. Parse it — give **conjugation**, **person-gender-number**, and **root**.
2. State the **semantic function** (Passive / Reflexive / Simple action — or "NOT Niphal" for distractors).

Distractors (14–16) are Qal verbs drawn from the same passages. Students learning to spot the Niphal must learn to distinguish it from the Qal active forms alongside it.

An answer key is at the bottom.

## Reflection Questions

1. Several Niphal forms of אָסַף in this passage describe Jacob's death (Gen 49:29, 33). What does the passive "to be gathered to one's people" suggest about ancient Israelite views of death?
2. In Gen 47:31, both הִשָּׁ֣בְעָה (imperative) and וַיִּשָּׁבַע (wayyiqtol) appear together. How does the narrative sequence reinforce the solemnity of Jacob's deathbed request?
3. Compare נִצָּבָה in Gen 37:7 (Reflexive — sheaf stands itself upright) with הַנִּצָּבִים in Gen 45:1 (Reflexive participle — servants stationed before Joseph). Is the reflexive force the same in both? What does each communicate about agency?
