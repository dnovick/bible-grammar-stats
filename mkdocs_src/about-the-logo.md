<h1>About the Logo</h1>

<img src="../assets/logo.png" alt="Berean Bible Bots logo" />
<p class="logo-caption">The Berean Bible Bots logo — spot anything unusual?</p>

<div class="logo-body">

<p>This logo was generated by an AI image model. It depicts a scholar at a desk, surrounded by ancient manuscripts, poring over what appears to be a biblical text — a fitting image for a project dedicated to studying the Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek scriptures.</p>

<p>There's just one small problem: <strong>the AI got a few things wrong.</strong></p>

<p>If you look closely, you'll notice that the text in the manuscripts and on the pages isn't quite... right. For example, the AI knows what ancient manuscripts are <em>supposed to look like</em> — it just can't actually read or write them.</p>

<p>Consider it a puzzle. Can you find all the errors? Is that Hebrew? Greek? Or something from a language that has never existed?</p>

<p>We think it's a fitting reminder for this entire project:</p>

<div class="disclaimer">
  <strong>AI is a powerful assistant — but it is not infallible.</strong><br>
  Every chart, analysis, exercise, paradigm, and piece of linguistic content on this site was generated by AI (Claude, Anthropic) under human direction. We have reviewed and tested the material, but errors will exist. The logo just makes that disclaimer visible and a little more fun.<br><br>
  <strong>Always verify what you read here against primary sources</strong> — the Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek texts themselves, and trusted grammars and lexicons. The Bereans did (Acts 17:11). You should too.
</div>
