# Ch10 Adjective and Number Drill — BBA Adjectives and Numbers

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Chapter 10*

---

## Instructions

Each item gives an Aramaic word or phrase from Daniel or Ezra. Your tasks:

1. Identify whether it is an **adjective** or a **number**.
2. For adjectives: give the **state** (absolute/determined/construct), **gender**, and **number**; then give the English gloss.
3. For numbers: give the **cardinal value** or **ordinal position**; then give the English translation of the phrase.

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 3, 14 show the adjective אָחֳרִי/אָחֳרִי modifying feminine nouns. What is the feminine singular absolute form of this adjective? How do you distinguish it from the masculine form?

2. Items 5 and 6 use the same adjective (רַב / קַדְמָי) in the determined state. What ending signals the determined state for masculine singular adjectives? How does this compare to the determined state ending on nouns?

3. Compare items 2, 7, 11, 13, 15, and 18 — all involve cardinal numbers with nouns. In items 2 and 15, שִׁבְעָה is a ms number. What gender are the nouns it accompanies? What does this tell you about the polarity rule for Aramaic numbers 3–10?

4. Item 4 uses חַד in the phrase "a statue." How can you tell from context whether חַד means "one" (the cardinal number) or "a" (the indefinite article)? Is there a grammatical difference?
