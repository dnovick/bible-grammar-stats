# Ch5 Determined State Forms Drill — BBA Nouns: Determined State

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Chapter 5*

---

## Instructions

Each item gives you an Aramaic noun in **one state** (absolute or determined). Write the noun in the **other state** in the blank column. The direction for each item is indicated in the Notes column.

Use the determined-state endings:

| Form | Abs. Ending | Det. Ending |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine singular | (none) | ָא- |
| Feminine singular | ָה- | ָתָא- |
| Masculine plural | ִין- | ַיָּא- |
| Feminine plural | ָן- | ָתָא- |

Vocabulary may be drawn from **Chapter 4** and **Chapter 5**.

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 3 and 8 (אֻמָּה, fs. abs. and אֻמְּמָן, fp. abs.) both produce determined forms ending in ָתָא-. If you encountered אֻמְּתָא in a text, what clues in the surrounding context would help you decide whether it means *the nation* (singular) or *the nations* (plural)?

2. Items 2, 6, 10, and 16 are all Chapter 4 vocabulary items used in determined-state forms. What does the distribution of determined-state Chapter 4 nouns in actual Daniel passages tell you about which state is more common in Biblical Aramaic narrative?

3. Item 11 (חֲמַר → חַמְרָא) shows a vowel shift: the patach under ח in the absolute becomes a qamets in the determined, while the mem's vowel disappears. Using what you know about stress and syllable structure, explain why the vowel pattern changes when ָא- is added.
