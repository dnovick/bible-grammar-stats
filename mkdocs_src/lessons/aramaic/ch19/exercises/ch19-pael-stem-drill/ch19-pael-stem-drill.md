# Chapter 19 — Pael Stem Drill

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Ch19: The Pael Stem*

---

## Instructions

For each numbered verb form drawn from Daniel or Ezra, provide:

1. **Conjugation** — Perfect, Imperfect, Imperative, Infinitive, or Participle (active or passive)
2. **Root** — the three root consonants (cite in Peal perfect 3ms form)
3. **PGN** — Person-Gender-Number where applicable (N/A for infinitive; active/passive for participle)
4. **Translation** — an English rendering appropriate to the stem and conjugation

**Quick diagnostics:**
- **Pael marker (all forms):** dagesh forte in R2 — the single most reliable Pael indicator
- **Perfect:** patach under R1 + tsere + dagesh under R2; 3ms = קַטֵּל pattern
- **Imperfect:** יְ- prefix (shewa, NOT hireq) — contrast Peal יִ-; full form יְקַטֵּל
- **Imperative:** same form as perfect 3ms (קַטֵּל) — no prefix; distinguish by context
- **Infinitive:** לְ prefix + -ָה suffix + dagesh forte + qamets in R2 (לְקַטָּלָה)
- **Active participle:** מְ- prefix + dagesh-tsere in R2 (מְקַטֵּל)
- **Passive participle:** מְ- prefix + dagesh-patach in R2 (מְקַטַּל)
- **III-he root:** imperfect ends in -ֵא; participle ends in -ֶה

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 1 (שַׁבַּח) and 3 (מְשַׁבַּח) both come from root שׁבח. They are in different conjugations but are both Pael. What specific features distinguish the perfect 3ms from the active participle ms? Which feature is shared by both?

2. Item 6 (בָּרֵךְ) and item 8 (מְבָרֵךְ) both come from root ברך. In Daniel 3:28, the form בָּרִיךְ appears as "Blessed is…" — that form is a **Peal passive participle** (qəṭīl pattern). Explain the morphological difference between בָּרִיךְ (Peal passive ptcp) and מְבָרֵךְ (Pael active ptcp). What single feature is the clearest marker of the Pael?

3. Items 10 (יְחַוֵּא) and 12 (נְחַוֵּא) are both Pael imperfect forms of חוה. (a) What do their prefixes tell you about PGN? (b) How does the III-he root affect the final form in both cases? (c) How would item 10 look if it were a Peal imperfect instead?

4. Item 14 (לְשַׁבָּחָה) is an infinitive. What three morphological features identify it as a Pael infinitive? How does it differ from a Peal infinitive construct?

5. Item 19 (מְקַטַּל) is a **passive participle** using the model root. How does it differ in vocalization from the Pael **active** participle מְקַטֵּל? What shared feature confirms both are Pael rather than Peal?

### Answers to Reflection Questions

**1.** The **shared feature** of שַׁבַּח (perfect 3ms) and מְשַׁבַּח (active participle ms) is the **dagesh forte in ב (R2)** — the universal Pael marker present in all conjugations. The **distinguishing features** are: (a) the perfect 3ms has **patach under R1 (שַׁ-)** with no prefix; (b) the active participle ms has the **מְ- prefix** (mem + shewa) before R1. The presence of מְ- is the unambiguous mark of the derived-stem participle; its absence with the same root pattern identifies the perfect or imperative.

**2.** The **Peal passive participle** בָּרִיךְ (from qəṭīl pattern: בְ + רִי + ךְ) has a **hireq-yod on R2** and a **vocal shewa under R1** — this is the passive state/result pattern. The **Pael active participle** מְבָרֵךְ begins with **מְ-** (the derived-stem participle prefix) and has **dagesh forte in ר (R2)** with tsere. The **clearest single marker** of the Pael participle is the **מְ- prefix** — no Peal form (active or passive participle) carries this prefix. Conversely, the Peal passive participle (qəṭīl) has no מְ- prefix and has the distinctive hireq-yod vowel pattern rather than dagesh-tsere.

**3.** (a) The prefix **יְ-** (yod + shewa) = 3ms imperfect; the prefix **נְ-** (nun + shewa) = 1cp imperfect. Both are Pael imperfect forms, distinguished only by the prefix consonant that signals PGN. (b) The **III-he root** חוה causes the final form of both to end in **-ֵא** (tsere + aleph mater lectionis) rather than a final strong consonant — the aleph is a historical mater lectionis for the long ē vowel of the III-he imperfect ending. (c) If item 10 were a **Peal imperfect**, the prefix vowel would be **יִ-** (hireq), not יְ- (shewa): the Peal form would be **יִחְוֵא** (or a related form depending on root analysis). The shift from יִ- to יְ- is the diagnostic that identifies the Pael in the imperfect.

**4.** Three features identify לְשַׁבָּחָה as a Pael infinitive: (a) the **לְ- prefix** (lamed + shewa) — the Aramaic infinitive prefix; (b) the **dagesh forte in ב (R2)** — the universal Pael D-stem marker; (c) the **-ָה suffix** (final he with qamets) — the Aramaic infinitive ending, plus the **qamets** under R2 (with dagesh) as opposed to tsere in the perfect/imperfect. A **Peal infinitive construct** would typically be **לְמִשְׁבַּח** (with mem infix and no dagesh forte in ב) — a completely different pattern. The Pael infinitive is unmistakable by the combination of לְ- prefix + dagesh in R2 + -ָה ending.

**5.** The **Pael passive participle** מְקַטַּל has **patach** under R2 (with dagesh forte): קַ**טַּ**ל — the doubling + patach vowel signals the passive. The **Pael active participle** מְקַטֵּל has **tsere** under R2 (with dagesh forte): קַ**טֵּ**ל — the doubling + tsere signals the active. The **shared feature** confirming both are Pael is the **מְ- prefix** (derived-stem participle marker) combined with the **dagesh forte in R2**. No Peal participle carries מְ- prefix; no Peal form has unexplained dagesh forte in R2.
