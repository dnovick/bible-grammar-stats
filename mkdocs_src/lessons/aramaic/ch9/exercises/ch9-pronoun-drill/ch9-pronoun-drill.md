# Ch9 Pronoun Drill — BBA Pronouns

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Chapter 9*

---

## Instructions

Each item gives an Aramaic word or short phrase from Daniel or Ezra. Your tasks:

1. Identify the **type** of pronoun (personal, demonstrative, interrogative, existential, relative/genitive, indefinite).
2. Give the **person, gender, and number** (where applicable).
3. Give the **English gloss or translation**.

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 2, 11, and 8 are all demonstrative pronouns. Which ones are near ("this") and which are far ("that")? Is there any overlap? What is the plural "these" form?

2. Items 3 and 7 are related existential particles. What is the relationship between אִיתַי and לֵית? Where do you find both in Daniel 3 in close proximity?

3. Item 17 (כִּדְנָה) is a compound form built on the demonstrative דְּנָה. What does the prefix כְּ- add to its meaning? Can you identify other compound forms built on דְּנָה in Daniel?

4. Items 4 and 18 both involve the word מַן. How does adding דִּי (item 18) change its grammatical function? Give an example sentence illustrating each use.
