# Ch7 Preposition Drill — BBA Conjunctions and Prepositions

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Chapter 7*

---

## Instructions

Each item gives an Aramaic phrase from Daniel or Ezra containing a preposition or conjunction. Your tasks:

1. Identify the **preposition or conjunction** and give its **gloss**.
2. Identify the **noun/object** that follows (where applicable) and its **state** (absolute, determined, or construct).
3. Give the **English translation** of the complete phrase.

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 4 and 20 both use the preposition בְּ-, but the nouns they prefix to are in different states. How can you tell the difference between **בְּמַלְכוּתָא** ("in the kingdom") and **בְּמַלְכוּ** ("in a kingdom")? What is the significance of the ָא ending?

2. Items 2 and 19 both express "from," but one uses מִן as a stand-alone word and one uses the prefixed form מִ-. What causes the nun to assimilate in item 19? Why did it not assimilate in item 2?

3. Item 13 uses the compound כָּל-קֳבֵל דִּי. What are the two elements that make up this compound? What function does דִּי serve here? Compare this to the use of דִּי in item 17 (עַד דִּי).

4. Items 8 and 9 use לָהֵן and הֵן respectively. Both are particles rather than prepositions. Based on the context in Daniel, what logical relationship does each particle introduce in its clause?
