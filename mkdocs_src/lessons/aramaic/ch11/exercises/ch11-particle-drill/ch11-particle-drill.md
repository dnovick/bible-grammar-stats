# Ch11 Particle Drill — BBA Adverbs and Particles

*Basics of Biblical Aramaic, Van Pelt — Chapter 11*

---

## Instructions

Each item gives an Aramaic word or phrase from Daniel or Ezra containing an adverb or particle. Your tasks:

1. Identify the **adverb or particle** in the phrase.
2. Give its **category** (time, place, manner, negation, discourse marker, causal/purpose).
3. Give the **English gloss or translation** of the adverb/particle (or the complete phrase where appropriate).

## Reflection Questions

1. Items 1 and 12 both feature אֱדַיִן. This particle appears more than 50 times in Daniel alone. How does it function differently at the beginning of a major narrative scene vs. within a sequence of quick actions? Can you find two examples in Daniel 3 or 6 that illustrate the difference?

2. Items 2 and 14 both express negation but use different particles (לָא vs. אַל). What is the Hebrew equivalent of each? What kind of verbal form does each typically negate in Biblical Aramaic?

3. Item 9 (לָהֵן) can mean "therefore," "but," or "except." In Daniel 2:6 (לָהֵן חֶלְמָא וּפִשְׁרֵהּ הַחֲוֻנִי), which meaning applies and why?

4. Items 6 and 18 both express "thus/in this manner" (כֵּן and כִּדְנָה). Are these interchangeable? Look at two passages in Daniel where each appears and describe whether you notice any stylistic difference in their usage.
