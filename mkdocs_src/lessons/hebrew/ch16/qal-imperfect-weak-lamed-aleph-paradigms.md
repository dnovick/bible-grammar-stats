# Qal Imperfect — III-א (Lamed-Aleph) Paradigms

*Full inflection tables for III-א weak roots in the Qal Imperfect.*
*The aleph at R3 quiesces; the preceding vowel lengthens.*

---

## III-א: מָצָא (find) — Imperfect

| Person | Form | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3ms | יִמְצָא | Qamets before silent aleph; long form |
| 3fs | תִּמְצָא | Same pattern |
| 2ms | תִּמְצָא | Identical to 3fs |
| 2fs | תִּמְצְאִי | Shewa under aleph before hireq-yod suffix |
| 1cs | אֶמְצָא | Aleph prefix; qamets before silent aleph |
| 3mp | יִמְצְאוּ | Shewa under aleph before -וּ |
| 3fp | תִּמְצֶ֫אנָה | Tsere before aleph + נָה |
| 2mp | תִּמְצְאוּ | Same as 3mp |
| 2fp | תִּמְצֶ֫אנָה | Same as 3fp |
| 1cp | נִמְצָא | Nun prefix; qamets-aleph |

> **Key:** The Jussive 3ms is **identical** to the long form (יִמְצָא) — III-א does *not*
> shorten in the Jussive. This distinguishes it from III-ה.

## III-א vs. III-ה — Jussive Contrast

| Form | III-א (יִמְצָא) | III-ה (יִבְנֶה → יִבֶּן) |
|---|---|---|
| Long Imperfect 3ms | יִמְצָא | יִבְנֶה |
| Short Jussive 3ms | יִמְצָא (no change) | יִבֶּן (ה drops) |

> This distinction is important: a form ending in א is either III-א long Imperfect OR Jussive
> (they are identical). A form ending in ֶן or ֶם is typically a III-ה Jussive.
