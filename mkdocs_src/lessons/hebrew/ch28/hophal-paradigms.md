# Hophal Paradigm Tables — הוּמַת / יֻקַּם

*Hophal paradigm root: מוּת (to die → to be put to death) — strong biconsonantal, paradigm of the passive causative*
*Alternative root: נָקַם (to avenge → to be avenged) — demonstrates the u-vowel under prefix*

## Imperative

The Hophal has no Imperative. Passive verbs cannot be commanded.

| Form | **Hophal** |
|---|---|
| ms | **מֻגָּד** |
| mp | **מֻגָּדִים** |

> **Key marker — Hophal Participle:** מ prefix + **Shureq (וּ) or Qibbuts (ֻ) under R1** + Qamets (ָ) or Patach under R2. Distinguished from Pual Participle (which also has מ prefix + u-vowel) by: Hophal Participle is the **passive of the Causative** (Hiphil); Pual is the **passive of the Intensive** (Piel). Both use מ + u-class vowel under R1.

## Summary: Hiphil vs. Hophal at a Glance

| Feature | Hiphil (active causative) | **Hophal (passive causative)** |
|---|---|---|
| Perfect prefix | הִ (Hireq) | **הוּ or הֻ (Shureq/Qibbuts)** |
| Imperfect prefix vowel | יַ / תַּ (Patach) | **יוּ / יֻ (Shureq/Qibbuts)** |
| Wayyiqtol prefix | וַיַּ | **וַיּוּ** |
| Participle prefix | מַ (Patach) | **מוּ / מֻ (Shureq/Qibbuts)** |
| Imperative | Yes (הַקְטֵל) | **No — passive** |
| Meaning | Causes action | **Passive: caused to undergo action** |

**The single most reliable Hophal identifier: u-class vowel (Shureq וּ or Qibbuts ֻ) under the prefix consonant.**
