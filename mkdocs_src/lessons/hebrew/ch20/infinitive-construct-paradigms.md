# Qal Infinitive Construct Paradigms

*BBH Chapter 20 · Reference file*

---

## Qal Infinitive Construct — Strong Roots

The IC does **not** inflect for person, number, or gender. It has one form (plus variants with preposition/suffix).

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ prefix | + בְּ prefix | + כְּ prefix |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| שמר (guard) | שְׁמֹר | לִ/שְׁמֹר | בִּ/שְׁמֹר | כִּ/שְׁמֹר |
| כתב (write) | כְּתֹב | לִ/כְתֹב | בִּ/כְתֹב | כִּ/כְתֹב |
| זכר (remember) | זְכֹר | לִ/זְכֹר | בִּ/זְכֹר | כִּ/זְכֹר |

> **Key marker:** Strong IC = shewa under R1 + holem on R2. Identical to Imperative 2ms. Preposition לְ takes hireq (לִ–) before the IC. Context distinguishes IC from Imperative.

## Qal Infinitive Construct — III-ה Roots

The final ה is replaced by **וֹת** (holem-waw + taw):

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| ראה (see) | רְאוֹת | לִ/רְאוֹת | Tsere often appears: לִרְאֹת |
| עשה (do) | עֲשׂוֹת | לַ/עֲשׂוֹת | I-gutt. chateph under ע |
| היה (be) | הֱיוֹת | לִ/הְיוֹת | Hateph-seghol under ה |
| הלך (go) | — | לֶ/כֶת | I-י replaces III-ה entirely; special form |
| שתה (drink) | שְׁתוֹת | לִ/שְׁתּוֹת | |
| בנה (build) | בְּנוֹת | לִ/בְנוֹת | |
| ידה (praise) | הוֹדוֹת | לְ/הוֹדוֹת | Hiphil of ידה (more common) |

> **Key marker:** וֹת ending (holem-waw + taw) is the diagnostic for III-ה IC. It looks like a plural noun ending — do not confuse! The context (verbal behavior, following an object or subject) confirms IC.

## Qal Infinitive Construct — Biconsonantal

Full long vowel is preserved in all biconsonantal IC forms:

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| בוא (come) | בֹּא | לָ/בֹא | Holem-waw; aleph quiesces |
| שוב (return) | שׁוּב | לָ/שׁוּב | Full shureq |
| קום (arise) | קוּם | לָ/קוּם | Full shureq |
| מות (die) | מוּת | לָ/מוּת | Full shureq |
| שים (put) | שִׂים | לָ/שִׂים | Full hireq-yod |
| ריב (contend) | רִיב | לָ/רִיב | Full hireq-yod |

> **Key marker:** Biconsonantal IC retains the full long vowel of the root. The lamed prefix takes qamets (לָ–) before most biconsonantals.

## Qal Infinitive Construct — I-י

I-י roots show **contracted** forms in the IC, often with a seghol-taw ending:

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| הלך (go) | לֶ/כֶת | לֶ/כֶת | No separate bare form common; the ל is fused |
| ישב (sit/dwell) | שֶׁ/בֶת | לָ/שֶׁ/בֶת | Seghol + bet + taw |
| ידע (know) | דַּ/עַת | לָ/דַ/עַת | Patach + ayin + taw |
| יצא (go out) | צֵאת | לָ/צֵאת | Tsere + aleph + taw |
| ירד (go down) | רֶ/דֶת | לָ/רֶ/דֶת | Seghol + dalet + taw |
| ירש (inherit) | רֶ/שֶׁת | לָ/רֶ/שֶׁת | |
| ילד (give birth) | לֶ/דֶת | לָ/לֶ/דֶת | |

> **Key marker:** I-י IC forms often end in **ת** (taw), giving them a distinctive noun-like appearance. לֶ/כֶת is the most common; the original yod/ה is absent.

## Qal Infinitive Construct — I-נ

Nun assimilates in some roots; others are regular:

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| נתן (give) | תֵּת | לָ/תֵת | Both nuns drop; very short |
| נסע (set out) | נְסֹ֣עַ | לִ/נְסֹ֣עַ | Nun retained (not assimilated in IC) |
| נגש (approach) | גֶּשֶׁת | לָ/גֶּשֶׁת | Nun drops; seghol-taw |
| נשא (lift) | שְׂאֵת | לָ/שְׂאֵת | Nun drops; tsere-taw |
| נפל (fall) | נְפֹל | לִ/נְפֹל | Nun retained |
| נגד (tell) | הַ/גִּיד | לְ/הַ/גִּיד | Hiphil more common for this root |

> **Key marker:** תֵּת (נתן = "to give") is one of the most striking IC forms — only two consonants visible. The I-נ root drops or assimilates the nun. Contrast with I-נ roots where nun is retained (נְסֹ֣עַ).

## Qal Infinitive Construct — I-aleph

| Root | Bare IC | + לְ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| אמר (say) | אֱמֹר | לֵ/אמֹר | Hateph-seghol; quotative formula |
| אכל (eat) | אֱכֹל | לֶ/אֱכֹל | Hateph-seghol under aleph |
| אהב (love) | אֱהֹב | לֶ/אֱהֹב | |
| אסף (gather) | אֱסֹף | לֶ/אֱסֹף | |

> **Key marker:** I-aleph IC has hateph-seghol under the aleph (composite shewa). The quotative לֵאמֹר (from אמר) is the most frequent IC form in the entire Hebrew Bible.
